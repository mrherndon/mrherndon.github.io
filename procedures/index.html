Classroom Proceedures

  1. Once you enter class, place your bag against the wall and take your supplies to your desk.
  2. Unless they are used for the warm-up, chromebooks should be on the floor near your feet.
  3. If posted, begin working on your warm-up.
  4. You will be considered tardy if you are not in class by the time I shut the door. Tardy students are to sign in on the clipboard upon entering the room and bring me an appropriate pass from a teacher or administrator.
  5. If you need to use the bathroom, wait for an appropriate time to ask permission, and sign out before exiting the classroom
  6. If you finish your classwork early, you may complete any of the following: study for future assessments, complete assignments that are not yet due, or complete missing assignments.
  7. Continue working until I dismiss you.
  8. Before exiting the class, put up all materials used during this class period.

Classroom Expectations

  1. Be READY. Be on time to class and in your seat when the bell rings, focused on my instructions.
  2. Be RESPONSIBLE. Bring completed assignments, notebook, calculator, pencil, and your charged chromebook to class each day. Be an active participant in all class activities and discussions. (SUCCESS = EFFORT)
  3. Be RESPECTFUL. Respect all people and property. Be cooperative and not destructive. Follow all school-wide rules.

Supplies

Required:

  • Paper - graph or dot, can be a notebook or in a binder
  • Pencils and erasers
  • A colored pen

Optional (but highly suggested):

  • Colored Pencils
  • Straight Edge
  • Dry Erase Markers
  • Post-It Notes
  • Glue Sticks
  • Highlighter
  • Scissors
  • Earbuds (bring them with you!)
  • Graphing Calculator (TI-84)

Calculators

A classroom set of calculators is available for students to use. Each student will be assigned a calculator to use in the classroom; however, these calculators must not leave with students. I expect students to treat these calculators as their own property. Students in Algebra I will not check out a graphing calculator from the math department. There are websites (Desmos!) and graphing calculator apps available for download that students can use at home.

*If students plan to take upper-level math courses, I recommend buying their own graphing calculator: TI-84+ (rechargeable calculators are available).

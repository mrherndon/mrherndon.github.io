Grading Policies

Grading polcies are subject to change.
All changes will be reflected on this page, with the date the change was made.

Unassisted Assignments (70% of semester grade)

Unassisted assignments will consist of unit assessments, district assessments and a semester exam. Unit assessments will have optional retakes, which students must take on their own time before the given deadline. If a student misses a test, it is their responsibility to coordinate a time to make up the test. Until the test is made up, a zero (N) will be recorded in the grade book. If a student misses the review day, they are still expected to take the test on test day. District assessments and the semester exam will not have optional retakes.

Assisted Assignments (30% of semester grade)

Assisted grades may include the following: checkpoints, exit tickets, class work, assignments, projects, warm-ups, group activities and reviews. If a student misses class, it is their responsibility to find out what activities were missed in class and complete any missed assignments.

* Assignments will not be accepted after the unit test.
* Checkpoints will have an optional retake that must be taken on students’ own time. Students must demonstrate relearning of the material was attempted.

Grade Book Codes

X – Exempt (assignment does not need to be turned in)
N – Not Completed (assignment has not been completed but may be made up)**
Z – Zero (assignment is too late for credit and cannot be made up for a grade)**
0 – Zero (actual grade on assignment)
Blank – (assignment has not reached its due date and is not calculated in the average OR grades for the assignment have not yet been entered)

**These gradebook codes will count against a students’ final exemption.

Zero/Missing/Late Work Policy

Assisted assignments are due as assigned and missing assignments will be accepted until the unit assessment.

Retest Policy

In order to retake a test, students will need to do a retest assignment and turn in all missing work for that unit. Retests must be completed before the next unit assessment and must be completed outside of class.
